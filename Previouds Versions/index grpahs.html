The relationship economy is here

There are now new expectations for businesses and their customers, no longer are they seen as adversaries but as aligned collaborators where the risks are shared and the rewards are not zero sum. An emphasis on long term value instead of short term gains with transparency between parties is now a fundamental belief in modern businesses.

The challenge

The more relationships a business builds with their customers, the less value that they can generate from each relationship. This means that businesses have focussed on maximising value with a limited number of key customer relationships.

For each new relationship that is made, the value a business can offer to all their existing relationships should also increase. In order for this to happen, businesses must rethink their processes and adopt something new.
